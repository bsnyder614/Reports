What the Audit Found

Four plain-language findings, then three moves to make — in order.

Finding 01

You are known when searched by name — invisible when searched by need.

When a buyer already knows your name and looks you up, AI confirms you clearly and consistently. Your NMLS number is indexed. Your Equity Resources bio pulls up. Experience.com shows a 5-star rating with 29 verified Zillow reviews. That branded presence is real and solid. But when a Newark buyer opens ChatGPT cold and asks "who's the best loan officer near me?" — your name does not appear. Discovery presence sits at 6%. That's the game you're not in yet, and it's where buyers are being sent to someone else without you ever knowing.

Finding 02

You have a genuine lane — but you have not claimed it.

Your bio says you help buyers get from "can I buy?" to "here are the keys" — and your reviews confirm it. First-time buyers show up in review after review. Buyers who didn't know the steps. Buyers who needed someone to make the process feel possible. That is a lane: the loan officer for buyers who feel stuck, overwhelmed, or like homeownership is out of reach. But your bio headline, your Facebook page, and your Zillow listing all say something slightly different. AI needs one clear message repeated in many places to start recommending someone. Right now it sees a warm, competent generalist — not the go-to name for any one buyer type in Newark.

Finding 03

Your reviews are your strongest asset — but they are locked in one place.

Twenty-nine Zillow reviews, all 5 stars, many with real detail: names, situations, timelines. That is genuinely strong for a market like Newark. The problem is that almost all of it lives on Zillow and feeds through to Experience.com — but there is no confirmed Google Business Profile review presence, no BBB review base, and no Evidence that AI is citing your review content in discovery answers. Reviews only help AI if they are spread across the platforms AI actually reads. A borrower telling Zillow you made them feel at ease is great. That same borrower telling Google is what gets you into an AI answer for "best loan officer in Newark."

Finding 04

Your two profile pages are not doing the same job.

You have two Equity Resources bio pages — callequity.com and callequitymortgages.com — that are nearly identical. That duplication does not hurt you, but it does not help either. Neither page is written the way AI likes to quote: there is no FAQ section, no local market data, no specific numbers. Your bio tells a great personal story, but AI needs answer-first structure to know what to pull and repeat. A few quick rewrites — leading with your lane, adding real local facts, building out a Q&A — would turn these pages from good bios into pages AI wants to cite.

04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Newark," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Brach Fabbro exists. It does not yet know he is Newark's go-to loan officer for first-time buyers who feel stuck.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You don't win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍

✍️

How much the web talks about you, and whether it's good: reviews, listings, local news, partner pages. Almost no loan officer works on this, and it's the strongest engine.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

This is your floor — and it is already solid. Twenty-nine Zillow reviews, all 5 stars. Borrowers who describe you as patient, communicative, fast, and calm under pressure. Reviews that mention specific situations: first-time buyers with no realtor, buyers on tight timelines, buyers in other time zones. That is the kind of review language AI loves to cite. The only problem: it is almost entirely on Zillow. That is one platform. AI pulls from many.

What Helps

  • Add Google to every post-close review ask. You already have the Zillow habit — just extend it.
  • A review that says "Brach helped me buy my first home in Newark in 21 days, zero stress" is worth ten stars-only reviews.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real and active.
  • Make sure your loan types show up by name in reviews: FHA, USDA, VA, construction, first-time buyers. The words matter.
  • Keep the cadence going. A few new reviews each month beat 100 old ones.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that attention is wasted if there is nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That is the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a clear, plain answer, then add more detail. "Looking for a first-time homebuyer loan in Newark, Ohio? Here's how it works."
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No giant blocks of text.
  • Add a Q&A. Real buyer questions with short answers. "Do I need 20% down?" "Can I use USDA in Licking County?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real numbers. Local facts, typical close times, Licking County market specifics. Things AI cannot make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I've helped over 100 Central Ohio families buy their first home. Here's what they usually worry about first."
  • Show your credentials. Photo, NMLS number, years at Equity Resources — prominently.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it's the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans; you do not run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not one big push you will never keep up.
  • Approval. Some posts need a compliance check. Build that relationship now. Make approved templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change yourself on your Equity Resources pages and what you need to request from the marketing team.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and reputation take months. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: You have held processor, LOA, trainer, and LO roles at the same company for over a decade. You know how to learn a system and run it consistently. That is exactly what this requires.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it's free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Newark OH," "Best mortgage lender Newark OH," "First-time homebuyer help Licking County Ohio," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI used — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it's the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #1816349 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
