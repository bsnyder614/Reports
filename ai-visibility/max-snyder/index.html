04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Cincinnati," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Max Snyder and the 100 Doors Lending Team exist. It does not yet know you are Cincinnati's go-to loan officer for educational, client-first homebuying.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍
Be Found
AI searches the web to answer. If you are not in the results, you are not in the answer. The older kind of Google work still matters — it is the starting point.
✍️
Be Quotable
Write your pages in a clear, simple way AI likes to copy: short lines, real numbers, and real stories from Cincinnati buyers you have helped.
💬
Be Talked About
How much the web talks about you, and whether it is good — reviews, listings, local news, partner pages. This is the strongest engine and where almost no loan officer works.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do: the loans you offer and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat 200 old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close. Make it part of your team's close-out checklist.
  • Get them where AI and buyers look: your personal Google Business Profile, Zillow (your individual profile), and Experience.com.
  • Ask for specifics, not just stars. "Max walked us through every step of a first-time purchase in 24 days" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real, active, and responsive.
  • Make sure Conventional, VA, FHA, and DPA programs show up by name on your pages — those are the queries buyers use.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Ask honestly and make it easy — that is the whole system.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the everyday grind of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You manage a team, close loans, and run events. The fix is baking the review ask and monthly AI check into your existing team process — not adding a new project.
  • Approval. RWM has a compliance process. Build the relationship now. Make approved templates once, so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change on your own profile and what needs RWM to update at the company level. Some wins are in your hands today.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and outside proof take months. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: You already run workshops, manage a team, and produce content. That infrastructure is exactly what this requires. Add one layer at a time.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Cincinnati OH," "Best mortgage lender Cincinnati," and "Educational mortgage lender Cincinnati OH."
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI used — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

Toolkit B

Rules to Follow

AI visibility is marketing, and you work in a regulated field. General guidance — not legal advice. Check your plans with your compliance team.

AreaWhat to Keep in Mind
NMLS LicenseShow your NMLS number in ads where required. Keep your name, company, and license consistent everywhere. Only advertise in states where you are licensed: OH, KY, MI, and SC.
RESPADo not pay, or get paid, just for referrals. Shared ads with agents or builders must be paid for fairly, for real work. This area is watched closely — get it reviewed.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help — not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #2217562 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. The 100 Doors education-first approach is yours. AI needs to be able to find and repeat it.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
