04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Cincinnati," it doesn't guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge portions of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Tyler Mitchell exists and works at Sibcy Cline. It does not yet know she is the go-to loan officer for Cincinnati's military families and first-time buyers.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do multiple independent websites say the same specific thing about you?

You don't win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you — across reviews, partner pages, community mentions, and your own bio.

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI doesn't read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion from what borrowers say about what it's actually like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat a hundred old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close. Make it part of your process, not an afterthought.
  • Get them where AI and buyers look: Google Business Profile and Zillow. Add Experience.com as a third platform.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Helped us close our first home in 28 days despite a complicated situation" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you're real and active.
  • Ask reviewers to mention specific loan types: first-time buyer, VA loan, refinance. That language feeds your discovery presence.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy for people to click the link.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that outside attention is wasted if there's nowhere compelling to send a buyer. Your own pages are still among the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That is the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Lead with your lane. Your bio should open with your most specific, credible identity — not a generic welcome statement.
  • Answer first. Start each section with a clear plain answer, then add detail.
  • Add a Q&A section. Real buyer questions with short answers. "How does a VA loan work in Ohio?" or "What do I need to qualify as a first-time buyer in Cincinnati?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real details. Cincinnati market specifics, typical timelines, loan types you've worked. Things AI can confirm and cite.
  • Make your story visible. Your Navy service, Realtor background, seven-day availability, and family-style care should all be in the first two paragraphs — not buried.
  • Show credentials clearly. Photo, NMLS #1606914, and your history at Cendant, AmSouth, and CrossCountry tell AI you have deep roots in this industry.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails isn't the plan. It's the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans and you're available seven days a week. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not a one-time marketing push you'll never keep up.
  • Approval. Some content may need a compliance check. Build that relationship now. Approved templates mean you stop re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can update yourself on your Sibcy Cline profile versus what needs corporate to change. LinkedIn and Google are always yours to control.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix your bio and AI may cite it within days. But reviews and community mentions take months. The people who win don't quit in week three.
Your edge: Five years in the Navy means you know what it looks like to show up every day with discipline, even when results aren't immediate. This plan requires exactly that.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your intake forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of what got you there.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude in fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Cincinnati OH," "VA loan lender near Cincinnati," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did AI say about you?
  • Note which websites AI used as sources — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. Direction matters more than any single number.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up in normal Google results. Still matters — it's the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is largest.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — positive or negative — that the web conveys about you, mostly through reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that isn't yours — like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important assets for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #1606914 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value for real work.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan advertising rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add required disclosures.
