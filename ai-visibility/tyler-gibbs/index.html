04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Westerville," it doesn't guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on large portions of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Tyler Gibbs exists at Fairway in Westerville. It does not yet know Tyler Gibbs is the specific, trusted local expert a first-time buyer or move-up buyer should call.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do multiple independent websites say the same true thing about you?

You don't win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans and Service

AI doesn't read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do and what it is like to work with you. Your Experience.com reviews already contain the right ingredients. The job is getting them to the platforms AI indexes most heavily.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat a hundred old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close. Build a post-close routine and stick to it.
  • Get them where AI and buyers look first: Google Business Profile, Zillow, and Experience.com.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Tyler walked us through every step and was at the closing table" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real, active, and care about the people you serve.
  • Make sure your loan types show up by name in your bios: conventional, FHA, VA, first-time buyer programs.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Ask honestly, make it easy, and let the real experience do the work.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans; you do not run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not one big push you will never keep up.
  • Approval. Some posts need a compliance check. Build that relationship with your branch coordinator now. Make approved templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can update yourself on your Fairway pages and what requires a request through the corporate system.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast. Fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and reputation take months. The people who win do not quit in week three.

06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere: on your forms and out loud on calls. "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of what caused that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude in fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Westerville OH," "Best mortgage lender Westerville OH," and your personal lane queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did AI say about you?
  • Note which websites AI used as sources. Those are your next Layer 3 targets.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction matters more than any single result.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI: tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters as a foundation.
AI Visibility
How often and how well AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. Eight in ten AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name: "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — positive or negative — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours: Zillow, Nextdoor, a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS 1981786 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning payment for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan advertising rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
