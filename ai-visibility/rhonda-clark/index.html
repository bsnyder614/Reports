What the Audit Found

Four plain-language findings, then three moves to make — in order.

Finding 01

Strong when found, invisible when searched.

When someone already knows your name and looks you up, AI confirms you clearly. NMLS lookup, direct name searches, and brand queries all return consistent results across tools. But when a Columbus buyer opens ChatGPT and asks "best loan officer near me" — your name does not surface. Discovery presence is at 5%. That is the whole game you are not yet in, and it is the gap this playbook is built to close.

Finding 02

Your message says "everyone" when AI needs "someone."

Your bio leads with "I enjoy assisting homebuyers in financing their first home and next homes" — a warm statement, but a generalist one. Your product page lists down payment assistance, VA, FHA, USDA, jumbo, bank statement, physician, and foreign national loans. That breadth is a real strength with borrowers who come to you. But AI cannot repeat a generalist message. It needs one clear thing to say about you. Columbus is a competitive market with hundreds of loan officers. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over someone else.

Finding 03

Third-party proof lives in one channel and does not travel.

Your strongest external presence is through Coldwell Banker Realty — your name appears on multiple office pages across Central Ohio as the preferred lender, which is genuine third-party signal. But that signal is buried inside a real estate brokerage site, and AI does not weight it heavily as a standalone endorsement. No Zillow lender profile was found. No Google Business Profile reviews were confirmed. No Experience.com activity was visible. The Coldwell Banker relationship is a real asset that needs to be built on, not relied on alone.

Finding 04

LinkedIn is active but not doing the strategic work.

Your LinkedIn profile shows consistent posting activity — market updates, rate commentary, seasonal buyer tips. That kind of regular presence is genuinely more than most loan officers maintain, and it shows up in search results. But the profile itself still reads as a general mortgage professional in Columbus rather than the go-to lender for a specific buyer type. The activity is there. A sharper headline, a clearer "known for" statement, and posts that connect back to your specific lane would turn that existing activity into real discovery traction.

Starting in mortgage origination in 1993 means you have seen every market — the boom, the crisis, the recovery, the rate spikes. 04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Columbus," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Rhonda Clark exists. It does not yet know she is Columbus's go-to lender for a specific buyer type.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍
Be Found
AI searches the web to answer. If you are not in the results, you are not in the answer. The older kind of Google work still matters — it is the starting point.
✍️
Be Quotable
Write your pages in a clear, simple way AI likes to copy: short lines, real numbers, and your own true stories from Columbus borrowers.
💬
Be Talked About
How much the web talks about you, and whether it is good — reviews, listings, local news, partner pages. Almost no loan officer works on this, and it is the strongest engine.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do: the loans you offer and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat 200 old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close.
  • Get them where AI and buyers look: Google Business Profile, Zillow, and Experience.com.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Closed our loan in 21 days even with a complex file" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real and active.
  • Make sure your loan types show up by name in reviews: first-time buyer, physician loan, bank statement, jumbo, VA, FHA.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that attention is wasted if there is nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That is the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a clear, plain answer, then add more detail.
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No giant blocks of text.
  • Add a Q&A. Real buyer questions with short answers. "How do physician loans work in Ohio?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real numbers. Local facts, typical close times, Columbus market specifics. Things AI cannot make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I have helped hundreds of Columbus buyers close loans since 1993, including many through Coldwell Banker's Central Ohio offices."
  • Show your credentials. Photo, NMLS number, 30 years of experience — prominently.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans; you do not run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not one big push you will never keep up.
  • Approval. Some posts need a compliance check. Build that relationship now. Make approved templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change yourself on your Guaranteed Rate Affinity profile and what needs to go through corporate.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and reputation take months. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: 30 years in this business means you know how to build a system and work it through any market. That is exactly what this requires.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Columbus OH," "Best mortgage lender Columbus OH," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI used — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

Rules to Follow

AI visibility is marketing, and you work in a regulated field. General guidance — not legal advice. Check your plans with your compliance team.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #657898 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
