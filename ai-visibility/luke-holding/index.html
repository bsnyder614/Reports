04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Toledo," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Luke Holding exists. It does not yet know he is Toledo's go-to loan officer for any specific type of buyer.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do many websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Right now, the review signal for Luke Holding personally is close to zero. One reference in a colleague's review is not enough. This is the single fastest way to move your score.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat 200 old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close. You have the communication skills — make the ask feel like a natural part of closing.
  • Get them where AI and buyers look: Google Business Profile, Zillow, and Experience.com.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Luke closed our loan in 18 days and walked us through every step" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real and active.
  • Make sure your loan types show up by name in reviews: FHA, VA, conventional, new construction, first-time buyer.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that visibility effort is wasted if there is nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That is the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a clear, plain answer, then add more detail. "Luke Holding helps Toledo buyers close in as little as two weeks" is better than "Welcome to my mortgage page."
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No giant blocks of text.
  • Add a Q&A. Real buyer questions with short answers. "What does pre-approval actually take?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real numbers. Local Toledo facts, typical close times, market specifics. Things AI cannot make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I have helped 200+ buyers in the Toledo area through the pre-approval process." Your seven years at GVC give you real numbers to use.
  • Show your credentials. Photo, NMLS number, GVC role — prominently and consistently.

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans; you are not running a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not one big push you will never keep up. Your marketing background means you already know this.
  • Approval. Some posts and pages need a compliance check. Build that relationship now. Make approved templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company context. Know what you can update yourself on your GVC profile and what needs to go through the team. The Toledo branch page is a company asset — your Zillow and Google profiles are yours.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and reputation take months. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: Seven years at one company and a background in digital marketing mean you understand how systems work and how audiences find things. That is exactly what this plan requires.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Cannot See It, You Cannot Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your intake forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Toledo OH," "Best mortgage lender Toledo OH," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI used — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is adequate.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, a local news site, or a partner page. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #1900201 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
