04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Parma," it doesn't guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Matt Wisniewski exists. It doesn't yet know you're Parma's go-to loan officer for first-time buyers or Heroes program borrowers.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of different websites say the same thing about you?

You don't win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI doesn't read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI recommends your name, it forms an opinion about what it's like to work with you. Right now, that opinion is good but narrow. It only reaches as far as Ruoff's own website.

What Helps

  • Get reviews on Google Business Profile and Zillow — those are where AI searches independently of your company site.
  • Ask every borrower the same way after every close. Make it a routine, not a request.
  • Ask for detail, not just stars. "Closed in 18 days with a self-employed buyer" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. It shows AI — and buyers — that you're real and active.
  • Your Ruoff page testimonials referencing the Heroes program and small-business buyers are strong. Ask those borrower types specifically for Google and Zillow reviews.
  • Claim your Birdeye listing and start directing post-close reviews there as a secondary platform.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Ask honestly after every close and make the link easy to find.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Your Own Pages

All that attention is wasted if there's nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That's the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a plain answer to a real buyer question, then add detail.
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No giant blocks of text.
  • Add a Q&A. Real buyer questions with short answers. "What down payment help is available in Cuyahoga County?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real numbers. Local close times, Parma market specifics, down payment program amounts. Things AI can't make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I've helped first-time buyers in Parma and Broadview Heights navigate down payment assistance programs."
  • Show your credentials. Photo, NMLS #2090169, specific programs you're certified in — prominently.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails isn't the plan — it's the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not one big push. Ten minutes after each close asking for a review beats a one-time campaign you'll never repeat.
  • Approval. Some content needs compliance review. Build that relationship now and make approved templates once so you're not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change yourself on your Ruoff bio and what needs to go through corporate. The LinkedIn headline and Google Business Profile are fully yours to update today.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. Reviews and outside proof take months. The people who win don't quit in week three.
Your edge: The reviews on your Ruoff page already show someone who follows through. Borrowers describe fast responses, clear guidance, and closings that happened on time. That's the system this plan needs. You already have it.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it's free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Parma OH," "Best first-time homebuyer lender near Parma," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites AI cited — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI: tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it's the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name: "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that isn't yours: Zillow, Nextdoor, a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #2090169 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
