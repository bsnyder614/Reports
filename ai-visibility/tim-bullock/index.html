04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Akron," it doesn't guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Tim Bullock exists. It doesn't yet know he's Akron's go-to loan officer for first-time buyers who ask a lot of questions.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You don't win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you — in the same words, on the right platforms.

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI doesn't read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. The good news: you already have the reviews. The job is moving them to the right platforms.

What Helps

  • Redirect the post-close review ask to Google first, Zillow second. Keep the same cadence you already use.
  • Ask for specific language: "Closed our FHA loan in 21 days" beats five stars with no text. You already get great written reviews — keep that going.
  • Reply to every Google and Zillow review. AI sees that you're real and active.
  • Make sure your loan types show up by name in reviews and on your pages: FHA, VA, USDA, down payment assistance, 203K. These are the phrases buyers search for.
  • Keep reviews fresh. A few new reviews each month beat 200 old ones.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy — which you already know how to do.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails isn't the plan — it's execution. You run a team. That's an advantage here. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You don't need to do this yourself. The content work on myfirsthomeohio.com, the profile updates, the review redirect — these can be delegated to a team member or handled in batches. Own the strategy. Delegate the execution.
  • Approval. Some content changes need compliance review. Build that relationship now. Create approved templates once so the team isn't re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change directly on your UHM profile versus what requires a request to corporate. The standalone site gives you more flexibility.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins come fast — update a page and AI may quote it within days. The review platform shift will take 60 to 90 days to show up. The people who win don't stop in week three.
Your edge: 30 years of building a system and a team that runs it. That's exactly what this requires — and you already know how.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your intake forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you'll know the engine is working. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app in a fresh window and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and costs nothing.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows, not the same session.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Akron OH," "Best first-time homebuyer lender Akron Ohio," and "Best FHA loan officer near Akron."
  • For each: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI cited — those are your next Layer 3 targets.
  • Track the trend month over month. Direction is what matters, not a single result.

08 · Rules to Follow

Toolkit B

Rules to Follow

AI visibility is marketing, and you work in a regulated field. General guidance — not legal advice. Check your plans with your compliance team.

AreaWhat to Keep in Mind
NMLS LicenseShow your NMLS number in ads where required. Keep your name, company, and license consistent everywhere. Only advertise in states where you're licensed.
RESPADon't pay, or get paid, just for referrals. Shared ads with agents or builders must be paid for fairly, for real work. This area is watched closely — get it reviewed.

One rule that covers most of this: Build your name on things that are true, clear, and said the same way everywhere. True keeps you legal. Clear is what AI copies. Same everywhere is what AI trusts.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it's the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is among the strongest in this audit series.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is the gap this playbook is designed to close.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews. Yours is excellent. The platform distribution is what needs work.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that isn't yours — like Zillow, ThreeBestRated, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search — and the highest-priority move in this playbook.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #233777 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. You have a clear one: first-time homebuyers in Akron for 30 years. The job is saying it the same way everywhere.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way — which is what myfirsthomeohio.com needs.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value for real work.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
