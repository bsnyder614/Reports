04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Maumee," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are, but only if enough places online talk about you the same way. Right now, AI knows Amy Brentlinger exists and works at Capital Home Loans. It does not yet know you are the go-to loan officer for buyers who need patience and a steady hand.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you, the good and the bad, into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews, and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat 180 old ones sitting in one place.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close.
  • Get them where AI and buyers look beyond your own site: Google Business Profile, Zillow, and Experience.com.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Stuck with me for months until I found the right house" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real and active.
  • Make sure your approach shows up by name: patient, steady, first-time buyer friendly.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy.

Layer Two

Your Name & Brand

Then Say It the Same Way Everywhere

Quick test: If you asked a past client, a Realtor partner, and an AI app "what is Amy Brentlinger known for?", would all three give the same answer? If not, this is the fix.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that attention is wasted if there is nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal is for AI to name you and link to your page. That is the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a clear, plain answer, then add more detail.
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No giant blocks of text.
  • Add a Q&A. Real buyer questions with short answers. "How long can you work with me while I look for the right home?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real numbers. Local facts, typical close times, Maumee market specifics. Things AI cannot make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I've helped first-time buyers in the Maumee and Perrysburg area take their time and find the right home."
  • Show your credentials. Photo, NMLS number, years of experience, prominently.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan. It is the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans. You do not run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not one big push you will never keep up.
  • Approval. Some posts need a compliance check. Build that relationship now. Make approved templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change yourself and what you need to request through Capital Home Loans.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast. Fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and reputation take months. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: Your reviews already show you stick with people through a long process. That same patience is exactly what this plan requires from you.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere, on your forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question, where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT," you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude, fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Maumee OH," "Best mortgage lender Maumee OH," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI used. Those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

Rules to Follow

AI visibility is marketing, and you work in a regulated field. This is general guidance, not legal advice. Check your plans with your compliance team.

AreaWhat to Keep in Mind
NMLS LicenseShow your NMLS number in ads where required. Keep your name, company, and license consistent everywhere. Only advertise in states where you are licensed.

One rule that covers most of this: Build your name on things that are true, clear, and said the same way everywhere. True keeps you legal. Clear is what AI copies. Same everywhere is what AI trusts.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI: tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters. It is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is strong.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name, like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling, good or bad, that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours, like Zillow, a review site, or a local news page. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #1582010 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
