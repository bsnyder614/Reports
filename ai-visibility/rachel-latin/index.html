The lane exists. The signal does not yet match.

04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Wadsworth," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on large portions of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Rachel Latin exists. It does not yet know she is Wadsworth's go-to loan officer for first-time buyers and OHFA programs.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages at once, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Send every borrower a direct Google review link after close. That is the single highest-leverage action in this playbook.
  • Keep the ask consistent: same message, every time, after every close.
  • Request reviews on Google Business Profile and Zillow — the two platforms AI uses most.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Rachel got us into our first home with OHFA help — we were approved in under 30 days" is worth ten five-star ratings with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real, active, and engaged.
  • Make sure your loan types appear by name in reviews: FHA, VA, USDA, OHFA, first-time buyers, Heroes program.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Ask honestly and make it easy for borrowers to say yes.

Your Own Pages

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan. It is the everyday friction of actually doing it. Four points of friction, and how to beat each one.

  • Time. You close loans — you do not run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not one big push you will never maintain.
  • Approval. Some posts and content need a compliance review. Build that relationship now. Create approved templates once so you are not re-checking every single post.
  • Company pages. Know what you can update yourself on rachellatinloans.com and LinkedIn versus what requires a request through Fairway corporate. Prioritize the pages you control.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — update a page and AI may quote it within days. But Google reviews and reputation compound over months. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: You already have a review-gathering system that produced 130-plus reviews. That discipline is the hardest part. Redirect it to Google and Zillow and the results follow.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your intake forms and out loud on discovery calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" or "Google AI mentioned you" — you know it is working. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh sessions each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Wadsworth OH," "OHFA loan officer near Wadsworth," and "first-time homebuyer help Wadsworth Ohio."
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI cited — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. Direction is what matters, not a single snapshot.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Nextdoor, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS 542872 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
OHFA
Ohio Housing Finance Agency. The state program that provides down payment assistance, reduced rates, and special programs like OHFA Heroes for first-time buyers.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
