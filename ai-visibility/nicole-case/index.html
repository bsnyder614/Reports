02 · Findings & Next Moves

Your Personalized Diagnosis

What the Audit Found

Four plain-language findings, then three moves to make — in order.

Finding 01

Confirmed when known. Invisible when discovered.

When someone already knows your name and looks you up, AI finds you. NMLS number searches and direct name queries return your Huntington profile and FastExpert listing consistently. But when a Toledo buyer opens ChatGPT and types "best loan officer near me" or "mortgage help in Sylvania" — your name does not appear. Discovery presence is at roughly 3%. That is the entire game you are not yet in, and it is where the buyers who have never heard of you are deciding who to call.

Finding 02

Your positioning is broad where AI needs it to be specific.

Your bio on both the Huntington site and FastExpert describes a wide range of loan products: conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, jumbo, doctor loans, construction-to-permanent, renovation, and down payment assistance. That is an impressive toolkit. But AI does not recommend generalists — it recommends specialists. There is no single thing Nicole Case is known for across your indexed presence. Picking one clear lane (first-time buyers, doctor loans, or down payment assistance programs are your strongest options in Toledo) and saying it everywhere would give AI something specific to repeat. Right now it has nothing to anchor to.

Finding 03

Reviews and third-party proof are nearly absent.

No confirmed reviews were found on Zillow, Google Business Profile, or Experience.com under your name. FastExpert has a profile but shows no client reviews. The Huntington bio page does not display individual LO reviews. AI builds its opinion of a loan officer almost entirely from what other websites say about you — and right now, very little is being said. This is the fastest thing to fix, and it has an outsized impact on where AI puts you. A consistent post-close review request sent to every borrower would begin changing this picture within 60 to 90 days.

Finding 04

The Huntington brand carries weight — but it covers you up.

When AI searches for "Nicole Case Huntington mortgage," it finds you — but it finds Huntington Bank first. As an LO at a major bank, your personal digital footprint is partially obscured by the institutional presence of your employer. Your Huntington bio page is live and indexable, but it carries Huntington's branding and design — not yours. Your FastExpert profile exists but has no reviews and limited content. Building your personal presence outside of Huntington's domain (LinkedIn, Google Business Profile under your own name, reviews on third-party platforms) is the path to being found as Nicole Case, not just as a Huntington loan officer in Northwest Ohio.

Your Edge

Toledo is an underserved market for AI visibility. You can own it.

Toledo is a real market with real buyers — and almost none of the loan officers here have built any AI presence at all. The expertise.com list of top Toledo mortgage brokers does not include a single individual LO profile with strong review volume and a clear specialty. The field is open. You have a legitimate product advantage in doctor loans, down payment assistance programs, and jumbo financing that very few Toledo LOs can match. You have been in this business long enough to know that first movers hold the ground. The LO who builds their AI presence in Toledo this year will be difficult to displace. That could be you.

04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Toledo," it doesn't guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on large parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Nicole Case exists at Huntington. It does not know she is Toledo's go-to loan officer for any specific type of buyer.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do multiple websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍
Be Found
AI searches the web to answer buyer questions. If you are not in the results, you are not in the answer. Traditional SEO is still the floor — it just is not the ceiling anymore.
✍️
Be Quotable
Write your pages in a clear, simple way AI likes to copy. Short lines, real numbers, and specific stories about Toledo and Sylvania buyers you have helped.
💬
Be Talked About
How much the web talks about you, and whether it is good — reviews, third-party listings, local news, partner pages. Almost no Toledo LO works on this, which makes it the strongest engine available.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews regularly and keep them fresh. A few new reviews per month beat 200 old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close — make it a system, not a request you remember sometimes.
  • Focus on Google Business Profile and Zillow first. Those are where AI and buyers look.
  • Ask for specific detail, not just stars. "Nicole helped us close our first home in 28 days" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI registers that you are real, active, and engaged.
  • Make sure specific loan types appear by name in reviews: doctor loans, down payment assistance, FHA, VA, jumbo.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or offer anything of value in exchange. No fake reviews. Ask honestly after every close and make it simple for the borrower to say yes.
Layer 2 · Your Brand

Layer Two

Your Name & Brand

Ask AI about a loan officer and it will not just list their products. It will tell the buyer what that person stands for. If you are known for nothing in particular, AI has nothing to say — so it says someone else's name.

Pick Your Lane

You cannot be known for everything. Given your product mix and the Toledo market, here are the three strongest lane options for Nicole Case:

  • Doctor Loans and Professional Mortgages — Toledo has a significant medical community anchored by ProMedica, Mercy Health, and the University of Toledo Medical Center. Very few local LOs specialize in this. Huntington has the product and you have the platform.
  • Down Payment Assistance and First-Time Buyers — Toledo is an affordable market where first-time buyer programs have real impact. OHFA programs, Huntington's own assistance products, and local grants are a genuine differentiator if you become the person who knows them cold.
  • Jumbo and Move-Up Buyers in Sylvania and Ottawa Hills — Northwest Ohio's higher-end submarkets are underserved by LOs who speak the language of larger loans. Positioning around Sylvania and the Ottawa Hills corridor could own that tier.

Then Say It the Same Way Everywhere

Your LinkedIn headline, FastExpert bio, your Huntington profile summary, and the way Realtor partners describe you should all point to the same one thing. The signal is consistency. Drift is what keeps AI silent.

Quick test: If you asked a past client, a Realtor partner, and an AI app "what is Nicole Case known for?" — would all three give the same answer? If not, this is where to start.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that attention is wasted if there is nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That is the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a clear, plain answer to what the buyer is asking — then add detail behind it.
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No long blocks of text that AI skips over.
  • Add a Q&A section. Real buyer questions with short answers. "What is a doctor loan and do I qualify?" AI returns to these repeatedly.
  • Use real numbers. Local Toledo market data, typical close times, program limits. Things AI cannot make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I have helped first-time buyers in Sylvania close using Ohio's down payment assistance program." One true story beats ten feature lists.
  • Show your credentials. Your photo, NMLS number, years of experience — visible at the top, not buried at the bottom.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the everyday grind of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat each one.

  • Time. You close loans — you do not run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not a one-time sprint you never repeat.
  • Approval at a bank. Huntington has a compliance process. Build that relationship now. Identify one person in marketing or compliance who can be a fast track for personal content approvals.
  • Institutional vs. personal presence. Some things you can do on your own (LinkedIn, Google Business Profile under your name, FastExpert). Others need Huntington's sign-off. Know which bucket each item falls in before you start.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — update a page and AI may quote it within days. Reviews and reputation take months. The LOs who win are not the ones who tried the hardest in week one. They are the ones still doing it in month four.
Your edge here: You have been in retail mortgage lending long enough to know that systems beat sprints. Build a small routine and repeat it. That is how this works.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Cannot See It, You Cannot Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question to every new borrower conversation: "Quick question — where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" — you know the playbook is working. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI tool in a fresh window and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude in fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Toledo OH," "Best mortgage for first-time buyers Toledo," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI cited — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters more than the absolute score.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is moderate — better than nothing, but not where buyers who have never heard of you will find you.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is your biggest gap and your biggest opportunity.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — positive or negative — that the web shows about you, based mostly on reviews and mentions.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, a local news site, or a partner page. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important single actions for local AI visibility.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS 562454 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you instead of a generalist with more tenure.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the detail behind it. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning payment for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value for real services rendered.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan advertising rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add required disclosures alongside it.
