04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Columbus," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Lauren Warner exists. It does not yet know she is a Columbus loan officer people should call.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do many websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you — across profiles, reviews, and third-party sites.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍
Be Found
AI searches the web to answer. If you are not in the results, you are not in the answer. Profiles, listings, and indexed pages are the starting point.
✍️
Be Quotable
Write your profiles in a clear, specific way AI likes to copy: short lines, your lane stated plainly, real borrower outcomes, and local Columbus market details.
💬
Be Talked About
Reviews, third-party mentions, partner pages, and community coverage. AI trusts what other websites say about you more than what you say about yourself. This is the strongest engine.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI mentions your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do: the loans you handle and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews often and keep them fresh. A few new reviews each month beat a hundred old ones.
  • Ask every borrower the same way, every time, after every close — Google and Zillow first.
  • Ask for specifics, not just stars. "She closed my FHA loan in 19 days" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real and active.
  • Make sure your loan types are named in reviews and profiles: FHA, VA, conventional, relocation, first-time buyers.
  • Claim your Experience.com profile and route your Rocket Mortgage borrower survey responses there consistently.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or offer anything in exchange for them. Ask honestly, make it easy, and let the experience do the work.
Layer 2 · Your Brand

Layer Two

Your Name & Brand

Ask AI about a loan officer and it will not just list their loans. It will tell the buyer what that person stands for. If you are known for nothing specific, AI has nothing to say — so it says someone else's name.

Pick Your Lane in Columbus

Columbus is a large, diverse market with strong demand in several buyer categories. Given Rocket Mortgage's capabilities and the Columbus buyer landscape, these are the strongest lane options:

  • First-Time Homebuyers — Columbus has a large and growing population of young buyers who are actively researching online. A loan officer who clearly owns this category and explains the process in plain language will get recommended.
  • FHA and VA Loans — government-backed lending in a big metro is a high-volume category. Own it explicitly and AI will find you when buyers ask for it.
  • Relocation Buyers — Columbus draws significant corporate relocation traffic. Buyers moving from out of state search for trusted local guidance. A lane here has very little individual competition.
  • New Construction and Builder Loans — Columbus is one of Ohio's fastest-growing areas for new builds. Buyers navigating builder lenders often look for independent guidance. This lane is underserved by individual LOs in AI search.

Say It the Same Way Everywhere

LinkedIn headline, Zillow bio, Google Business profile description, and any Rocket Mortgage-permitted personal materials should all land on the same one sentence. Consistency is the signal AI reads. Drift is what keeps you invisible.

Quick test: If you asked three past borrowers "what did Lauren Warner specialize in?" — would they all give the same answer? If not, this is the fix.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan. It is the everyday friction of doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans; you are not running a marketing agency. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not one big push you cannot sustain.
  • Approval. Some content and profiles will need a compliance review. Build that relationship now. Get approved language templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what Rocket Mortgage permits individual loan officers to do on personal profiles and external platforms. Some of these moves need clearance before you start.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — a new profile can be indexed within days. Review volume and reputation take months. The LOs who win do not quit in week four.
Your edge: Working inside a high-volume lender means you have the transaction flow to build reviews fast — if you build the habit. Three closes a month with a consistent review ask is enough to move the needle inside 90 days.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question to every intake form and every first call: "Quick question — where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" or "you came up when I asked Google about Columbus lenders," you know AI is sending you business. Do more of what moved that needle.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app in a fresh window and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and costs nothing.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows every time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Columbus OH," "Best mortgage lender Columbus," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each result, note: did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites AI used as sources — those are your next Layer 3 targets.
  • Track the trend month over month. Direction is what matters.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in Columbus.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Right now this works, but only weakly.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name. "Best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is biggest.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you. Driven mostly by reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Yelp, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important starting points for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS 222073 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a profile so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent or builder partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
