04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer near Buckeye Lake," it doesn't guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are, but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Tony Guinta exists. It doesn't yet know you're the go-to local lender for Buckeye Lake and Newark families.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you, the good and the bad, into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You don't win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI doesn't read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews, and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do: the loans you offer and what it's like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Ask every borrower for a Google and Zillow review directly, not just through the employer-hosted widget.
  • Keep reviews fresh. A few new ones each month on the right platforms beats a long list on the wrong one.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Closed our loan in 8 days" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review you can. AI sees that you're real and active.
  • Make sure your loan types show up by name: FHA, VA, USDA, Conventional, first-time buyer programs.
  • Bring your Downtown Newark Association connection into the story: buyers trust a lender who's visibly part of the community.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy.
Layer 2 · Your Brand

Layer Two

Your Name & Brand

Ask AI about a loan officer and it won't just list their loans. It will tell the buyer what that person stands for. If you're known for nothing in particular, AI has nothing to say, so it says someone else's name.

Pick Your Lane

You can't be known for everything. Given your loan mix, your review history, and your community standing, here are the strongest lane options for Tony Guinta near Buckeye Lake:

  • First-Time Homebuyer Specialist: your reviews already skew toward buyers who felt guided step by step through their first purchase.
  • FHA & VA Government Loan Specialist: a lane that's clear, easy for AI to repeat, and matches your stated loan mix.
  • The Local Lender Who's Part of This Community: built around your Downtown Newark Association leadership, a genuine differentiator almost no competitor can claim.

Then Say It the Same Way Everywhere

Your Ruoff bio headline, LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram should all land on the same one thing. Saying the same thing is the signal. Drifting is what hurts you.

Quick test: If you asked a past client, a Realtor partner, and an AI app "what is Tony Guinta known for?" would all three give the same answer? If not, this is the fix.

Layer 4 · Your Own Pages

Layer Four

Your Own Pages

All that attention is wasted if there's nowhere good to send the buyer. Your own pages are still some of the most-quoted sources in an AI answer. The goal: AI names you and links to your page. That's the mention and the click.

Write the Way AI Likes to Quote

  • Answer first. Start each page with a clear, plain answer, then add more detail.
  • Break it up. Short lines, clear headings. No giant blocks of text.
  • Add a Q&A. Real buyer questions with short answers, like "Can I buy with an FHA loan in Licking County?" AI loves to copy these.
  • Use real numbers. Local facts, typical close times, Buckeye Lake and Newark market specifics. Things AI can't make up.
  • Tell your own stories. "I've helped dozens of Licking County families close in under two weeks."
  • Show your credentials. Photo, NMLS number, community role, prominently.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails isn't the plan, it's the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You close loans; you don't run a marketing team. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not one big push you'll never keep up.
  • Approval. Some posts need a compliance check. Build that relationship now. Make approved templates once so you're not re-checking every post.
  • Company rules. Know what you can change yourself on your Ruoff bio and what you need to request from corporate.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast, fix a page and AI may quote it within days. But reviews and reputation take months. The people who win don't quit in week three.
Your edge: You're already publishing blog content and staying active on social media. That habit is the hard part. This plan just points it in a sharper direction.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere, on your forms and out loud on calls: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT," you know AI is sending you business. Do more of that.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and it's free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude, fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Buckeye Lake OH," "Best mortgage lender Newark OH," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI used, those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

Rules to Follow

AI visibility is marketing, and you work in a regulated field. General guidance, not legal advice. Check your plans with your compliance team.

AreaWhat to Keep in Mind
NMLS LicenseShow your NMLS number in ads where required. Keep your name, company, and license consistent everywhere. Only advertise in states where you're licensed.
RESPADon't pay, or get paid, just for referrals. Shared ads with agents or builders must be paid for fairly, for real work. This area is watched closely, get it reviewed.

One rule that covers most of this: Build your name on things that are true, clear, and said the same way everywhere. True keeps you legal. Clear is what AI copies. Same everywhere is what AI trusts.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI, tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters, it's the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name, like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling, good or bad, that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that isn't yours, like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #2221486 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
