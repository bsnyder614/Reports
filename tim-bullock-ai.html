04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Akron," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Tim Bullock exists. It does not yet know that Tim Bullock is Akron's go-to lender for first-time buyers.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍
Be Found
AI searches the web to answer. If you are not in the results, you are not in the answer. The older kind of Google work still matters — it is the starting point.
✍️
Be Quotable
Write your pages in a clear, simple way AI likes to copy: short lines, real numbers, and your own true stories from Akron borrowers who were first-timers.
💬
Be Talked About
How much the web talks about you, and whether it is good — reviews, listings, local news, partner pages. Almost no loan officer works on this, and it is the strongest engine.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do: the loans you offer and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Move your review ask to Google and Zillow after every close. You already have 343 Birdeye reviews — now channel that momentum to the platforms AI reads most.
  • Keep reviews fresh. A few new ones each month beat 200 old ones. AI looks at recency.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. "Tim's team closed our first home in 24 days and explained every step" beats a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real and active.
  • Make sure loan types show up by name in reviews and bios: first-time buyers, down payment assistance, FHA, OHFA, 203k, VA.
  • The phrase "we communicate more than anyone in the business" is your service promise. Use it consistently — it is quotable and specific.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Just ask honestly and make it easy.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the everyday hassle of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to beat them.

  • Time. You run a team; you do not run a marketing department. The fix is a small repeatable routine, not one big push you will never keep up. The team can help: one person owns the review ask after every close.
  • Approval. Some content needs a compliance check. Build that relationship with your compliance contact now. Make approved templates once so you are not re-checking every post.
  • Consistency. The website rewrite and profile alignment can be done once. Then the only ongoing routine is the review ask — which your team can systematize.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — rewrite a page and AI may cite it within 30 days. Reviews and third-party mentions take 60 to 90 days to compound. The people who win do not quit in week three.
Your edge: You have a team. Most loan officers are trying to do this alone. You can delegate the review ask, the profile updates, and the content calendar to team members — and that is a real competitive advantage.
06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Can't See It, You Can't Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question to your intake process and your closing calls: "Quick question — where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" or "I Googled first-time buyer lender Akron and your name came up" — you know the work is paying off. Track those answers.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. It takes 20 minutes and it is free.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best first-time buyer lender in Akron OH," "Best loan officer for first-time buyers Akron Ohio," and "Down payment assistance Akron Ohio loan officer."
  • For each, note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites the AI cited — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters.

Toolkit B

Rules to Follow

AI visibility is marketing, and you work in a regulated field. General guidance — not legal advice. Check your plans with your compliance team.

AreaWhat to Keep in Mind
NMLS LicenseShow your NMLS number in ads where required. Keep your name, company, and license consistent everywhere. Only advertise in states where you are licensed.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up high in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is strong.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name — like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews. Yours is strong; it just needs to be in the right places.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important things for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS #233777 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. For Tim, that lane is already chosen: first-time homebuyers in Akron.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value.
OHFA
Ohio Housing Finance Agency. Administers down payment assistance and first-time buyer programs — the programs Tim's buyers use most.
