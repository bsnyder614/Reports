04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Marietta," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

AI was trained on huge portions of the internet. It already has some picture of who you are — but only if enough places online agree on the same story. Right now, AI sees a loan officer who moved from PrimeLending to Stockton, with a few strong reviews and a thin current web presence. It does not yet see a clear, confident picture of Max Fitzgerald as Marietta's go-to lender.

2. What it finds right now

When a buyer asks a question, AI also searches the live web, reads many pages at once, and looks for one thing above everything else: agreement. Do multiple websites say the same true thing about you? Right now, they do not.

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you — and keeps saying it.

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you do and what it is like to work with you. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. The reviews you have already earned tell a great story. The job now is to put them where it counts.

What Helps

  • Ask for a review after every single close. Same ask, every time. Make it a habit.
  • Direct borrowers to Google Business Profile and Zillow first. Those are where AI looks.
  • Ask for detail in the review, not just stars. "Max got our first home closed in 28 days" beats five stars with no text.
  • Reply to every review. AI sees that you are real, active, and engaged.
  • Keep reviews coming monthly. A few new reviews each month beat 50 old ones.
  • Make sure the loan types you work most show up by name in review text: VA loans, FHA, first-time buyer programs, OHFA, conventional.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Ask honestly after every close and make it easy for borrowers to follow through.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan stalls is not the plan — it is the everyday friction of actually doing it. Four friction points, and how to handle each one.

  • Time. You are originating loans, not running a marketing department. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not a big project you will never finish. Ten minutes after every close, one post per week, one monthly check. That is it.
  • Platform control. Some of the changes — like the noindex issue on your Stockton bio — require Stockton's help. Identify which items you can do yourself and which need a request to your branch or corporate, then handle them in separate tracks.
  • Compliance. Build a relationship with your compliance contact now. Get one or two approved post templates you can reuse without re-checking each time.
  • Patience. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. Reviews and reputation take longer. The loan officers who win are the ones still going in month four, not just month one.
Your edge: You already have the most important ingredient — borrowers who will go out of their way for you. Someone who attends a closing four days after surgery earns reviews for life. You just need to ask.

2. Run a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app in a fresh window and run your key queries. Write down what you see. It takes 20 minutes and costs nothing.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Marietta OH," "Best VA loan officer Marietta Ohio," and your lane-specific queries.
  • For each, note: did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which websites AI cited — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. Direction is what matters, not a single snapshot.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results. This is often where buyers stop reading.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of getting your pages to rank in Google. Still matters — it is the starting point for being found by AI too.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your area.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is decent. It is not where you need to grow.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help — not a name. "Best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is and where the biggest opportunity lives.
Noindex
A tag on a web page that tells Google and AI not to crawl or rank it. Your current Stockton bio page has this tag — which means it is essentially invisible to search.
Citation
A website AI used as a source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to point to it. That trust comes from having clear, consistent, factual content.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Google, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages because they are independent.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your contact info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important platforms for local AI search. You need to claim yours.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. Your NMLS #1872870 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you are licensed.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you specifically, instead of giving a generic answer.
Answer-First
Writing so the clear answer comes first, with details after. AI prefers to quote pages written this way because it can pull the answer quickly.
RESPA
Federal law that bans paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value for real work done.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal rules for loan advertising. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add required disclosures. Check with your compliance team.
