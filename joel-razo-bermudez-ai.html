How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best bilingual loan officer near Cleveland," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has a picture of who is out there. Right now, AI knows Joel Razo exists. It does not yet know he is the go-to bilingual mortgage professional for Hispanic families in Northeast Ohio.

Layer 1 · Your Service

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews. You have real testimonials from Spanish-speaking borrowers — that is a genuine asset. The goal now is volume, recency, and placement in the right locations.

What Helps

  • Ask every borrower for a Google and Zillow review after close — in their preferred language. A simple text message works.
  • Aim for a few new reviews every month. Recent reviews outweigh an old pile.
  • Ask for details, not just stars. A review that says "Joel helped me understand the entire process in Spanish and we closed in 28 days" is far more powerful than a five-star with no text.
  • Reply to every review — in the same language the reviewer used. AI sees that you are active and engaged.
  • Make sure loan types appear by name in reviews and on your pages: FHA, conventional, first-time buyer, refinance.
  • Ask your existing borrowers who have not yet reviewed you. The Zillow testimonials on your website belong on Zillow's public profile too.
Compliance note: Never pay for reviews or give gifts in exchange for them. No fake reviews. Ask honestly and make it easy — a short link sent by text right after closing works well.

Layer 5 · Getting It Done

Layer Five

Getting It Done

The top reason this plan fails is not the plan — it is the friction of actually doing it week after week. Four friction points and how to beat them.

  • Time. You are an active loan officer. The fix is a small repeatable routine — not one big push you will never keep up. Fifteen minutes a week on reviews and one monthly AI check is enough to move the needle.
  • Compliance. Any posts about rates or programs may need a compliance check. Build that habit now. Save pre-approved language for common posts so you are not starting from scratch every time.
  • Website access. Know who controls your personal site and what you can change yourself. The NMLS fix and meta description updates should be fast once you have access.
  • Sticking with it. Some wins are fast — fix a page and AI may quote it within days. Reviews and third-party mentions take months. The loan officers who win are the ones who do not quit in week three.
Your edge here: You built a personal website and a bilingual content strategy early in your career without anyone telling you to. That is exactly the kind of initiative this plan runs on.

Add one question to every new client conversation: "Where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on Google" or "ChatGPT gave me your name" — you know AI is sending you business. Track it. Do more of what caused it.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check — in Both Languages

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries in both English and Spanish. It takes 20 minutes and costs nothing.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Run English queries: "Best bilingual loan officer Independence OH," "Spanish speaking mortgage lender Cleveland OH."
  • Run Spanish queries: "Prestamista hipotecario que habla español Ohio," "Mejor oficial de préstamos Cleveland Ohio."
  • Note: Did you show up? Where? What did it say about you? What sites did it cite?
  • Track the trend month over month. The direction is what matters — not where you start.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links. Buyers use them in English and Spanish.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results. It is what most people read and stop at.
SEO
Search engine optimization. Work that helps your pages show up high in Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
Meta Description
A short summary hidden in a webpage's code that tells search engines and AI what the page is about. Your current site is missing these — they are easy to add.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market — in any language.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is solid.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help, not a name. "Bilingual mortgage lender Cleveland." This is where your gap is — and your biggest opportunity.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you. Mostly built from reviews. In both languages, for you.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it in an answer.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, a community directory, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important tools for local AI search — and currently unclaimed in your name.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS # 2172682 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you. Make sure your site matches it exactly.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Your lane is clear: bilingual English/Spanish mortgage professional in Northeast Ohio. The work now is making the web say that.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way. In both languages, the principle is the same.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with Realtor partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value for real work done.
