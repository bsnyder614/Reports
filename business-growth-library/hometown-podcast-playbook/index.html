00 · The Core Idea

Why This Works

You Can't Out-Spend the National Brands. You Can Out-Local Them.

The largest lenders and brokerages are winning the branding war with budgets no individual can match. Competing for awareness at the national level is a losing game. The opportunity is to compete where the giants can't at the level of a single town.

A national brand can't be the person who interviewed the local high school football coach, featured the new coffee shop on Main Street, and sat down with the top listing agent in the zip code. That market one specific community is the piece of ground they cannot take. Micro-branding is how an individual claims it.

01 · Define Your Strategy

Step One

Define Your Strategy

Niche down until it feels uncomfortable, then go one level deeper. "Local" is the advantage don't dilute it.

Audience
Who You're Talking To
Real estate agents, homebuyers, local business owners, and community influencers. Agents are the priority they are typically the single largest referral source. Build the show for them first.
Theme
What the Show Is About
Hyper-local real estate trends, community events, and business spotlights. A good test: if it wouldn't matter to someone three towns over, it's exactly right for this show.
Cadence
How Often You Publish
Weekly is the goal. Bi-weekly is the floor. Consistency beats production value every time. A steady, average show will outperform a brilliant one that publishes only when inspiration strikes.

Three Episode Formats

A
Solo
5–10 Minutes
Market updates, lending tips, community insights. The fastest, easiest type to publish. Great for staying consistent between guest episodes.
B
Guest
20–30 Minutes
Agents, brokers, business owners, city officials. This is the relationship engine. Every guest is a potential referral partner.
C
Q&A
15 Minutes
The questions buyers and sellers actually ask. Strong lead-generation material. Easy to repurpose as video clips and social posts.
02 · Build the Framework

Step Two

Build the Framework

Done and published beats perfect and parked. Use inexpensive gear, set it up once, and start.

Branding & Positioning

Show Name
"My Hometown" or "My Hometown: [City]"
Plant your flag in the title itself. Claim the local territory from the first moment someone reads your show name in a directory or search result.
Tagline
"The heartbeat of [Your City] one story at a time."
Cover art should be clean and clearly local your face plus a recognizable landmark works well. Build it in Canva or hire a freelance designer. Don't overthink it.

Recording Setup

What You Need Recommended Option
Microphone Audio-Technica ATR2100x (USB/XLR dynamic mic). Professional sound for around $100.
Recording Software Riverside.fm for remote interviews. GarageBand or Audacity for solo recording and editing.
Podcast Hosting Buzzsprout or Spotify for Podcasters. Distributes automatically to Apple, Spotify, and other directories.

Your Three Calls to Action Use One Every Episode

For Agents
"Want to be featured? Let's talk."
For Listeners
"Follow for the latest market insight in [City]."
For Buyers
"Need a mortgage game plan? Let's connect."
Every episode should make it obvious what to do next and how to reach you. A show without a clear call to action leaves its best opportunities on the table.
03 · The Content Engine

Step Three

The Content Engine

Build segments, not episodes. Repeatable formats compound over time one-off ideas drain energy and disappear.

Five Recurring Segments

1
Neighborhood Deep Dive
Market stats, top listings, and buyer trends for one specific area. Highly shareable with local buyers and agents alike.
2
Agent Spotlight
A top agent's story, strategy, and what's working in the market. Your most powerful referral-relationship builder.
3
Small Business Feature
The local coffee shop, gym, or boutique that gives the town its character. These guests bring their own audiences and their gratitude.
4
Hometown History
The stories and local lore that make people feel deeply connected to where they live. Builds community identity around your show.
5
Market Minute
A tight 5-minute update on rates and home sales. Easy to produce, always relevant. Strong material for social clips and email newsletters.
Interview Structure 20 to 30 Minutes
0–2 min
Open
Who you are and what the episode is about. Keep it tight listeners came for the guest.
2–7 min
Their Story
How the guest got started, and why they love the community. This is where the relationship warms up.
7–22 min
Main Discussion
Local insight, success stories, and practical tips for buyers and sellers. This is the shareable content.
22–25 min
Close
Key takeaways, your call to action, and what's next. Always end with a next step. Never leave without one.
Remember: The episode is the excuse. The relationship is the asset.
04 · The Guest & Network Engine

Step Four

The Guest & Network Engine

Most professionals ask agents for referrals. The stronger move is to feature them first give visibility before asking for anything.

Who to Feature In Priority Order

  • Priority 1
    Top-producing real estate agents the primary referral source. Start here. Feature their story before you ever ask for a referral.
  • Priority 2
    Mortgage-adjacent professionals title, inspection, insurance. Strong co-branding partners who can introduce you to their agent networks.
  • Priority 3
    Local business owners coffee shops, gyms, boutiques with audiences of their own. They share content widely and introduce you to new circles.
  • Priority 4
    City officials & chamber members credibility and community reach. Great for positioning you as a trusted local voice.
  • Priority 5
    Community influencers bloggers and local social personalities who already have audiences ready to meet you.
Outreach That Gets a Yes
"Hi [Name], I'm launching a local show called 'My Hometown' that highlights the people making [City] better. I'd love to feature you and your story. Would you be open to a quick chat?"
Keep it genuine. Personalize the opening line with something real about the person. The rest follows naturally.

Turn the Show Into a Pipeline

1
Give First
Feature guests before asking for anything. Generosity earns the right to follow up later. The episode is the gift.
2
Co-Brand
Create a clip the guest will share. Their audience meets you, with their implied endorsement. That's earned credibility you can't buy.
3
Follow Up
After the episode goes live, reconnect naturally.
"If your buyers ever need a straight answer on financing, send them my way."
This is the heart of the system. Rather than cold-calling for business, you build a roster of local professionals who have a relationship with you and have already introduced their audience to you on their own. The deals follow the relationships.
05 · Promotion & Distribution

Step Five

Promotion & Distribution

Record once, show up everywhere. A single interview can become five to ten pieces of content.

📱
Social Media
Instagram & Facebook Reels with captions and local hashtags. LinkedIn for professional takeaways. YouTube Shorts to drive traffic. Slice each episode into 5–10 clips.
📧
Email / CRM
A weekly "This Week in [City]" newsletter. A personalized thank-you and episode link to every guest they will often forward it to their own contacts.
📰
Local Media
Pitch local news sites and blogs for added reach. Partner with the local real estate board to cross-promote episodes to their agent membership.
The math works in your favor. One 25-minute guest episode can produce a full-length podcast, 5–8 social clips, 1 email newsletter segment, a guest highlight post, and a clip the guest shares with their own network all from a single conversation.
06 · Monetization & Lead Generation

Step Six

Monetization & Lead Generation

This is a business-development tool with a microphone attached. Measure relationships and deals, not downloads.

Strategy 1
Co-Brand with Agents
Co-host episodes and run joint marketing. Shared audience, shared upside. This deepens the referral relationship in both directions and gives agents a reason to keep coming back.
Strategy 2
Local Sponsorships
Title companies, home inspectors, and local businesses that want local reach will often pay to be featured or mentioned. Use this to offset production costs while strengthening your network.
Strategy 3
Lead Funnels
Point every episode to a free homebuyer guide, a rate consultation, or a quick mortgage game plan call. The show creates the trust. The CTA converts it into a conversation.
Pro Tip: Treat the podcast as the top of your funnel, not the product. Every episode creates a networking opportunity that can lead to real estate and mortgage deals. Download count is a vanity metric. The number of relationships started and deals influenced is the real measure of success.
07 · Your First 90 Days

The Launch Sprint

Your First 90 Days

Strategy needs a start date. This is a simple sequence to go from zero to a published, compounding show in one quarter.

Days 1–30 · Build
Lock the Foundation Before You Launch
  • Lock the name, tagline, and cover art.
  • Buy the microphone. Set up hosting and your show feed.
  • Record three episodes before publishing one a buffer protects consistency.
  • Write a list of ten dream first guests. Send the first five invitations.
Days 31–60 · Launch
Publish Weekly Without Exception
  • Publish Episode 1 on a fixed day and time. Never miss it.
  • Send five new guest invitations every week.
  • Cut 3–5 clips from every episode and post them to social.
  • Start the weekly "This Week in [City]" email.
Days 61–90 · Compound
Build Momentum and Track What Works
  • Lock in recurring segments and a posting schedule.
  • Ask every guest for one introduction to someone else you should feature.
  • Review which episodes opened real conversations do more of those.
  • Track relationships started and deals influenced, not downloads.

Quick Start: You don't need a perfect plan to begin. You need Episode 1 published and Guest 2 booked. Build it, then make it better.
