10-Minute AI Win · Recruiting Playbook

The Closing Table
Review Strategy

One Habit  ·  Compounding Visibility

Most loan officers ask for reviews too late.
The best moment is right after signing.

When the buyer has the keys in their hand, they are at peak excitement. That is the moment to ask — not hours later, not the next day.

01 · Why This Matters

The Opportunity

AI and Search Look for Trust Signals

Google reviews are one of the clearest public signals that buyers trust you. More reviews can improve all of the following:

Google Visibility
Local Search Ranking
AI Search Results
Consumer Confidence
Online-to-Lead Conversion
Referral Credibility
The window is short. Excitement fades within hours. Capture the review while the buyer is still in the room — before life gets in the way.
02 · The Setup

Simple Execution

Remove Every Obstacle

The fewer steps between the buyer and the review, the higher the completion rate. Friction kills follow-through.

Before You Walk In
Bring a Tablet. Load the Page.
Have your Google review page already open on an iPad or tablet before the closing starts. Do not make the buyer search for a link or navigate to anything. Hand them a device that is ready to go.
Backup Option
Create a QR Code
Print a small QR code card that goes directly to your review page. If the buyer prefers their own phone, this eliminates every additional step. One scan. Done.
03 · The Script

Word for Word

What to Say at the Table

Practice this until it feels natural. The ask should feel like a genuine favor request, not a sales move.

The Ask · Say This After Signing

"Congratulations again — I'm really excited for you."

"Before we wrap up, could I ask one quick favor?"

"A lot of buyers today research their loan officer online before they ever reach out. If you feel like we did a good job for you, would you mind taking two minutes to share your experience?"

"I have the review page pulled up right here to make it easy."

→ Hand them the tablet
After They Finish

"Thank you. I really appreciate that. It means a lot — and it helps future buyers know who they can trust."

Then present the closing gift. Keep the gift separate from the review — it is part of the closing experience, not a reward for leaving one.

04 · Team Execution

System Thinking

One Review Is a Moment.
A System Is an Advantage.

The goal is not a single five-star review. The goal is consistent execution that compounds over time into a profile your competitors cannot match.

Every Closing
No Exceptions
Every buyer. Every closing. Every time. Designate one person at each closing to own the review ask so there is no ambiguity about whose job it is.
The Compounding Effect
Six Months From Now
A steady cadence of authentic reviews builds a visibility profile that referral partners and future buyers notice. The loan officers with 200 reviews did not do 200 different things. They did one thing, 200 times.
Track it. Add a simple checkbox to your closing checklist: Review asked? Yes / No. Measure the habit before you measure the results.
05 · Action Steps

Before Your Next Closing

5 Steps to Start Today

You do not need a perfect system to begin. You need your review link and the script. That is enough to start.

  1. Copy your Google review link and save it to your phone and tablet browser favorites.
  2. Load the review page on your tablet before you walk into every closing.
  3. Create a QR code as a backup — print it on a small card to keep in your closing folder.
  4. Practice the script out loud twice. It should feel natural, not rehearsed.
  5. Ask at every closing. No exceptions. Consistency is the strategy.

Small habit. Big visibility.  The review page takes 90 seconds to load. The script takes 30 seconds to deliver. The compounding visibility lasts for years.

Build Trust
Before They Search.

Every closing is a chance to extend your reputation beyond the room. The buyers who find you next year will find your reviews first.

Ask every time. The habit compounds. The results follow.
