04 · How AI Picks

Understanding the Machine

How AI Decides Who to Recommend

When someone asks an AI app "best loan officer in Columbus," it does not guess. It does two things at once.

1. What it already learned

The app was trained on huge parts of the internet. It already has an idea of who you are — but only if enough places online talk about you. Right now, AI knows Mitchell Severance exists. It knows "Mortgage Mitch" is a brand. It does not yet know you are Columbus's go-to loan officer for buyers who want to build long-term wealth.

2. What it finds right now

It searches the web, reads many pages, and pulls together what they say about you — the good and the incomplete — into one answer. Then it looks for one thing above all: agreement. Do lots of websites say the same thing about you?

You do not win by writing one great page about yourself. You win when the whole internet says the same true thing about you. Your website already says it. Now you need everything else to say it too.

The Three Engines

Everything in this playbook drives one of three engines. Skip one and the machine stalls.

🔍
Be Found
AI searches the web to answer. If you are not in the results, you are not in the answer. Traditional SEO work still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
✍️
Be Quotable
Write your pages the way AI likes to copy — short lines, real numbers, real local stories from Columbus-area buyers who built equity through smart homeownership.
💬
Be Talked About
How much the web talks about you, and whether it is good — reviews, listings, local news, partner pages. This is the strongest engine, and the one most LOs ignore entirely.

Layer One

Your Loans & Service

AI does not read your rate sheet. It reads your reviews — and so does every buyer who asks about you. Before AI talks about your name, it forms an opinion about what you actually do and what it is like to work with you.

What Helps

  • Get reviews onto Google Business Profile first. That is where AI looks hardest for local loan officers.
  • Get them onto Zillow second. It is the first place buyers look when a name comes up from AI.

06 · Keep Score

Measure It

If You Cannot See It, You Cannot Win It

1. Ask Where They Came From

Add one question everywhere — on your forms and on every first call: "Quick question: where did you first hear about me?" When someone says "I found you on ChatGPT" or "Gemini suggested you" — you know AI is sending you business. Do more of what is working.

2. Do a Monthly AI Check

Once a month, open each AI app fresh and run your key queries. Write down what you see. Twenty minutes, free, and it tells you exactly where to focus next.

  • Open ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI, and Claude — fresh windows each time.
  • Ask: "Best loan officer in Columbus OH," "Best loan officer for wealth building in Columbus," "Mortgage Mitch Columbus Ohio," and your NMLS number.
  • For each: did you show up? Where? What did it say about you?
  • Note which outside websites AI cited — those are your next targets for Layer 3.
  • Track the trend month over month. Direction matters more than any single result.

10 · Glossary

Plain-English Reference

Words to Know

AI search apps
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, and Google's AI — tools that answer questions in plain words instead of just showing links.
AI Overview / AI Mode
The AI-written answer at the top of Google, above the normal search results.
SEO
Search engine optimization. The work of helping your pages show up in normal Google results. Still matters — it is the floor, not the ceiling.
AI Visibility
How often, and how well, AI apps mention and recommend you when buyers ask about loan officers in your market.
Zero-Click Search
When someone reads the AI answer and stops, without clicking any website. 8 in 10 AI-answered searches end this way.
Branded Search
When someone searches for you by name. They already know you exist. Your score here is strong.
Discovery Search
When a stranger searches for a type of help — not a name. Like "best loan officer near me." This is where your gap is.
Sentiment
The overall feeling — good or bad — that the web shows about you, mostly from reviews.
Citation
A website AI used and points to as its source. Being cited means AI trusted your page enough to reference it.
Third-Party Site
Any website that is not yours — like Zillow, Reddit, or a local news site. AI trusts these more than your own pages.
Google Business Profile
Your free Google listing with your info, map pin, and reviews. One of the most important factors for local AI search.
NMLS / Consumer Access
The national license system for loan officers. NMLS 493686 is your official ID. Consumer Access is the public lookup AI uses to verify you.
Your Lane / Niche
The one main thing you want to be known for. Your lane is Wealth Builder LO for Central Ohio. Picking a lane gives AI a reason to name you over a generalist.
Answer-First
Writing a page so the clear answer comes first, then the details. AI prefers to quote pages written this way.
RESPA
Federal law banning paying for referrals. Key when working with agent partners. Shared marketing must reflect fair value for real work.
TILA / Reg Z
Federal loan ad rules. If you list a specific rate or term in marketing, you may need to add disclosures.
